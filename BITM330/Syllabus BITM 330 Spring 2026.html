4. Course Description

This course offers an in-depth exploration of database design and development, covering key topics such as theory, modeling, normalization, management, and administration. Students will gain a comprehensive understanding of the role of databases within organizations and learn to create effective database solutions to address business challenges.

The course includes a significant hands-on component, requiring extensive use of computers for in-class activities, homework assignments, and projects. Tools such as Microsoft Access and Structured Query Language (SQL) will be utilized to design and develop business applications, providing practical skills for real-world application.

5. Course Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion of this course, students should be able to:

  1. Understand the Importance of Databases – Comprehend the role and significance of database management in organizational contexts.

  2. Analyze Database Systems – Discuss and critically evaluate database-driven information systems in business environments.

  3. Gain Proficiency in Tools – Demonstrate proficiency in using Microsoft Access for database development and management.

  4. Develop Practical Applications – Build a solid foundation for designing and implementing database applications tailored to business needs.

  5. Understand Key Roles – Appreciate and articulate the responsibilities of database designers and managers in organizational success.

9.4 Academic Integrity

Academic integrity is a fundamental expectation in this course and across the School of Business. Violations will result in serious consequences, including failure of the course or expulsion from the university.

Collaborative Learning vs. Unauthorized Assistance:
Collaborative learning is encouraged for discussions and understanding. However, all submitted work must be your own. Sharing answers or copying work, whether in quizzes, assignments, or exams, is strictly prohibited.

Integrity Violations Include:

  • Communicating with others during an assignment or quiz
  • Copying another student's work or providing your own work for copying
  • Submitting copied or unoriginal data, including content from online sources, as your own
  • Using unauthorized assistance for any graded component

For complete policies, refer to the University's Academic Integrity Policies.

Important Note: Violations will be reported to the Office of Conflict Resolution & Civic Responsibility. Sanctions may include suspension or expulsion from the university.
